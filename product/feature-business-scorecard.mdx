## Overview

The Business Scorecard scores a business's brand and online/social presence and presents the result to a prospect. It's the engine behind the **DIY → DFY upsell funnel**: the goal of the project is to test our ability to upsell DIY prospects into a Done-For-You (DFY) sales meeting.

There are two flavors:

- **Public** — shown to prospects (e.g. from a popup or landing page).
- **Private** — the internal/authenticated version.

## Where it fits in the funnel

A prospect (primarily DIY paid-search traffic) completes the scorecard, which both delivers value (their score) and qualifies them. One proposed flow holds the score back and routes the prospect to an SDR conversation — e.g. "Thank you, our team will be in contact to talk about your scorecard," or a Calendly embed to book a 15-minute call — which also buys time to assemble accurate sources before the score is shown. The final UX decision is still in flight.

Popup / landing-page placement was scoped to **DIY paid traffic** (e.g. `Blaze.ai/home`), explicitly *not* DFY pages, to avoid disturbing the working DFY funnel.

## How it works / data sources

The score is generated from a business's public footprint, including its **Google Business Profile (GBP)** plus social platforms. A planned step shows and confirms the socials being used (including GBP) **before** results, and lets the user add/edit socials and regenerate the score.

> Fallback sources matter: businesses **without a GBP** currently produce weaker results, so a manual social-platform entry flow is needed to make non-GBP businesses viable.

## Known issues & roadmap

- **Non-GBP fallback sources** — improve recommendations for businesses without a Google Business Profile via manual social entry. (Viability blocker.)
- **Confirm/edit-socials-before-results step** — show the socials in use, allow add/edit + regenerate, on both public and private versions.
- **PDF export** — export the scorecard as a PDF (requested by a client).
- **Popup placement** — finalize where the public scorecard popup appears in the DIY paid-traffic funnel.
